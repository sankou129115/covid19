Coronavirus disease 2019

Other names: COVID-19, Wuhan Acute Respiratory

It is an infectious disease caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). It was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, China, and has resulted in an ongoing pandemic. The first confirmed case has been traced back to 17 November, 2019. Traces of the virus have been found in December-2019 wastewater that was collected from Milan and Turin. As of 22 June 2020, more than 8.91 million cases have been reported across 188 countries and territories, resulting in more than 466,000 deaths. More than 4.4 million people have recovered.
